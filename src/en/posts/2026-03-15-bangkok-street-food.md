Bangkok doesn't just feed you — it overwhelms your senses in the most delicious way possible.

## The Smells Hit First

Before you even see the food stalls, you smell them. Grilled satay, sweet coconut milk, the sharp kick of fresh chili. Every street corner is a different flavor profile.

## My Top 5 Must-Try Dishes

1. **Pad Thai** from a street cart — crispy, tangy, and somehow always better at midnight
2. **Som Tum** (green papaya salad) — the perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy
3. **Mango Sticky Rice** — the dessert that ruined all other desserts for me
4. **Tom Yum Goong** — spicy, sour, and full of fresh prawns
5. **Boat Noodles** — tiny bowls, massive flavor

## Where to Eat

The best food in Bangkok isn't in restaurants — it's on the streets. Chinatown (Yaowarat) after dark is a foodie's paradise. The stalls near Tha Tien pier serve incredible boat noodles for 15 baht a bowl.

## A Lesson in Flavor

Thai cuisine taught me that the best dishes aren't about one dominant flavor — they're about balance. Sweet meets sour, salty meets spicy, and somehow it all works in perfect harmony.

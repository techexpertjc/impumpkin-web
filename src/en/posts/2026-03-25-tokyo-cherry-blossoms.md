The first thing that hits you stepping off the Narita Express is the energy. Tokyo pulses with a rhythm unlike any other city I've visited.

## Day 1: Shinjuku Gyoen

The park was overwhelming in the best possible way. Hundreds of cherry trees in full bloom, families laying out picnic blankets, the gentle sound of petals falling like pink snow.

## Day 3: Yanaka's Hidden Temples

Away from the tourist crowds, Yanaka feels like old Tokyo. Narrow streets, small temples tucked between houses, and cats everywhere. This is where I found the real soul of the city.

## The Food

From ¥150 onigiri at convenience stores to an unforgettable bowl of ramen in a tiny shop under the train tracks — Tokyo feeds you well at every price point.

## What I Learned

Japan taught me that beauty lives in the details. The way a shop owner wraps your purchase, the perfectly aligned tiles on a temple roof, the silence in a crowded train. Everything is intentional.

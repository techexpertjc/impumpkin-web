The Taj Mahal remains the ultimate highlight of my journey through India. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983, this 17-hectare architectural marvel in Agra was completed in 1648 after two decades of meticulous construction.

## A First Glimpse

For a traveler like me, it is not merely a white marble structure, but a profound symbol of eternal love that transcends centuries. As I walked through the grand gateway, the first glimpse of the Taj took my breath away.

## The Architecture

The symmetry is perfect. Every arch, every minaret, every garden path mirrors its counterpart. The white marble seems to change color with the light — rosy at dawn, brilliant white at midday, and golden under the setting sun.

## The Story Behind

Emperor Shah Jahan built this monument as a testament to his love for Mumtaz Mahal, his beloved wife who passed away during childbirth. It took over 20,000 artisans and craftsmen to bring this vision to life.

## Tips for Visitors

- **Best time to visit:** Early morning for fewer crowds and beautiful light
- **Entry fee:** Around 1,100 INR for foreign tourists
- **Photography:** Allowed outside, no tripods inside
- **Duration:** Plan at least 2-3 hours to fully appreciate the site

The Taj Mahal is more than a building — it's an emotion carved in stone.
